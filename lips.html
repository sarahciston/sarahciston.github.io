e-face

Both trivial and intense, GIF aesthetics call for language that doubts and shouts, exceeds its bounds, encroaches where it shouldn't, and Tumblrs onward. Here, "e-face" pushes the GIF toward the excess it encourages: too slow, too fast, too melancholic, too ecstatic, too emotional--in short, relentlessly feminine. "How is a girl like a GIF?" it asks. "Silent. We travel like currency." Eighteen sets of painted lips mouth lines of original poetry and loop alongside corresponding stanzas of text. When performed live, GIFs are projected on the body of the performer reading aloud. The work is influenced by Helene Cixous' ecriture feminine and explores the materiality of the looping, circulating GIF as a foil for the gendered body and disempowered voice. It is part of a series of text experiments about the intersections of technology and gender.

Featured as a video in UCSD Design Lab's Microvideo exhibition at Calit2, as well as a live performance at the UCSD 3x3 Gallery.
